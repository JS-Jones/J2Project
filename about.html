In my academic and professional experience, I've built this duality between Business and Cognitive Sciences, both anchored in Computer Science. Aside from my focus in MedTech, I hope to expand my impact to a Global Setting and bring a new wave of Innovation.

In the next 10 years (hopefully by 2035) I believe that ...

    • MedTech innovations will facilitate real-time health monitoring and early detection of diseases through wearable devices and biosensors.
       Ex) My Fitbit will not only record my 10k steps but also let me know if I need a doctor checkup
    • Advancements in telemedicine and remote healthcare technologies will make medical services more accessible, especially in underserved regions.
    • Neuroprosthetics or Brain Computer Interfaces (BCI) will better integrate with the human nervous system, thereby improving the quality of life for individuals with disabilities. This will be achieved through several key developments:
       - Enhanced Signal Processing: Advances in machine learning will improve the interpretation of signals.
       - New Biocompatible Materials will reduce the risk of rejection and increase the longevity of implants.
       - User-Centric Design: creation of devices that are tailored to individual needs.
       - Integration with technology like virtual reality (VR) and augmented reality (AR) for rehabilitation processes.
       - Shift of BCI Technology from R&D to Commercial applications.
